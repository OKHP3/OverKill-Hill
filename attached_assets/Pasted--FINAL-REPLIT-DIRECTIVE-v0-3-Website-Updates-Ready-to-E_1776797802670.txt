```html
<section id="visual-edition" class="article-section">
  <h2>v0.3 — The Visual Edition</h2>
  
  <p>The slide deck is now live.</p>
  
  <p>This is the first version of the article where the argument becomes a visual artifact instead of only a written one.</p>
  
  <p>The Council produced the diagrams.<br>
  The deck packages the field.<br>
  The polls let the audience score the work.</p>
  
  <p>That matters because the whole ROY argument depends on a simple question:</p>
  
  <p><strong>Did the diagram create enough clarity to justify the words that produced it?</strong></p>
  
  <p>The v0.3 release turns that question into a public test.</p>
  
  <h3>What v0.3 Adds</h3>
  
  <ul>
    <li>The Etch-AI-Sketch Council Diagramming Shootout deck (18 slides, square and wide formats)</li>
    <li>The V1 and V2 diagram voting bracket</li>
    <li>Public poll rounds for reader scoring</li>
    <li>A scoring model for comparing audience judgment, architect judgment, and Council judgment</li>
    <li>A setup for the final v0.3.x debrief before the series moves into the v0.4 Council-origin arc</li>
  </ul>
  
  <h3>Public Scoring Bracket</h3>
  
  <p><strong>V1 Heat A:</strong> ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity<br>
  <strong>V1 Heat B:</strong> Copilot, Notion, Replit</p>
  
  <p><strong>V2 Heat A:</strong> ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, Notion<br>
  <strong>V2 Heat B:</strong> ChatGPT Pro, Claude, Replit, Perplexity</p>
  
  <p>The audience vote will not be treated as the only answer.</p>
  
  <p>It will be treated as one signal.</p>
  
  <p>After the polls close, the scoring breakdown will compare three scoring lanes:</p>
  
  <ol>
    <li><strong>Audience scoring</strong> — what communicated best to human viewers</li>
    <li><strong>Jamie's scoring</strong> — architectural judgment against fidelity, clarity, and usefulness</li>
    <li><strong>Council-assisted scoring</strong> — model-assisted rubric review across the full field</li>
  </ol>
  
  <p>The gap between those scores is probably where the interesting lesson lives.</p>
</section>
```
