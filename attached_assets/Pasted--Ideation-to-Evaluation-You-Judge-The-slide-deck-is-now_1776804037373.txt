## Ideation to Evaluation: You Judge

The slide deck is now live, and that changes the status of the argument. Up to this point, this article has been doing the work in prose. v0.3 is the moment the experiment leaves prose-only mode and becomes a visible field for comparison, judgment, and public scoring. The Council produced the diagrams. The deck now puts them side by side. What had previously been a claim about diagramming can now be inspected, compared, and judged in public.

That shift matters because the whole ROY argument has always been about whether a visual earns the words that produced it. If a diagram cannot create enough clarity to justify the explanatory cost, it does not matter how polished it looks. It is still overhead. The visual edition lets that question stop being theoretical. Instead of asking readers to trust my conclusion, it lets them see the field, compare the outputs, and decide what actually communicates.

That is also why v0.3 becomes a public scoring launch instead of just a deck drop. The first-pass diagrams and the revised diagrams are now moving into audience adjudication. Round 1 splits into two heats: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity in one bracket; Copilot, Notion, and Replit in the other. Round 2 does the same with the revised field: ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, and Notion in one heat; ChatGPT Pro, Claude, Replit, and Perplexity in the other. The point is not tournament theater for its own sake. It is to create a readable structure for comparison and let the audience move from observation to judgment.

The audience vote, though, is not the final answer. It is one signal. After the polls close, I will compare three separate scoring lanes: what human readers thought communicated best, what I scored highest for clarity, fidelity, and usefulness, and what the Council itself surfaces when forced through a rubric. The slide deck makes the field visible. The polls move it into evaluation. The final debrief shows where human judgment, architectural judgment, and Council judgment converge and diverge.
