Ask Jamie

Your friendly mid-century AI helpdesk.

Think: vintage tech guy who actually listens. Ask Jamie is where you bring the messy context, and get calm, diagram-ready answers in return.

Inside the OverKill Hill stack, Jamie is your “phone a friend” for decision trees, tradeoffs and “what should I do next?” moments.

Open Ask Jamie Where does Jamie fit?

What Ask Jamie is for

Not every question fits neatly in a form field. Ask Jamie exists for the “can we just think this through together?” kind of problems — where you need translation, not just information.

Explain it, don’t mystify it

Jamie breaks down concepts in plain language, with mid-century clarity: diagrams when they help, analogies when they land, no jargon for its own sake.

Walkthroughs, not one-offs

Instead of dropping one answer and bailing, Jamie walks you through steps and branches: “If this is true, go here; if not, let’s pivot.”

Calm tone, even when stakes are high

The visual and verbal tone is intentional: muted teals, paper grain, steady pacing — all designed to make hard conversations feel more navigable, not more chaotic.

Where Ask Jamie sits in the ecosystem

OverKill Hill P³ is the forge. Glee-fully is the joyful daily driver. Ask Jamie is the bench stool next to the workbench — the conversation that helps you pick the right tool in the first place.

  • Inside OverKill Hill: Jamie helps you think about system design, prompt strategy and where automation genuinely belongs.
  • Across Glee-fully: “Which Tool or Tool-ette should I use for this?” is a perfectly valid Ask Jamie prompt.
  • For you personally: career forks, learning paths, communication drafts, decision tradeoffs — the stuff you’d normally bother a trusted coworker for.
“I have three options and all of them feel risky. Can you help me see the tradeoffs?”
“Absolutely. Let’s map the options like a mid-century flowchart: goals at the top, constraints to the side, then branches. We’ll make the tradeoffs visible so the choice feels less foggy.”

Got a “what should I do next?” kind of question?

That’s an Ask Jamie moment. Bring the context, half-formed thoughts and constraints. Jamie will bring the diagrams, decision trees and a little mid-century calm.
